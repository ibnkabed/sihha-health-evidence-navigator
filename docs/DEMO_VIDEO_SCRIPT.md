# Public demo video script — target 2:50

## 0:00–0:18 — Problem

**Voiceover:** “A smartwatch can collect thousands of health measurements, but most people still cannot explain what changed or what to ask at their next appointment. Sihha turns scattered evidence into a private, understandable conversation brief.”

Show the dense Overview page, its seven familiar project sections, and the Safe synthetic-data badge.

## 0:18–0:48 — Apple Health ZIP

**Voiceover:** “On iPhone, Health can export a compressed `export.zip`. Sihha opens that ZIP inside the browser, reads `export.xml`, and extracts steps, sleep, resting heart rate, exercise minutes, and workouts. The file is never uploaded.”

Show the **استيراد Apple Health ZIP** button, then open Activity using the synthetic demo.

## 0:48–1:20 — Smartwatch activity

**Voiceover:** “The Activity page replaces disconnected numbers with a seven-day pattern. It adds a non-medical Today score, shows what changed, and states how complete the evidence is. Making progress visible gives people a reason to keep moving, exercise more consistently, and pay closer attention to their health — without claiming causation or medical readiness.”

Show the Activity modal with the KPI strip and several of its eight real charts: heart rate, sleep, steps, resting heart rate, distance, workouts, pace, and workout heart rate.

## 1:20–1:42 — Labs and supplements

**Voiceover:** “Lab results are shown as trends, not isolated red flags. Each result keeps the reference, latest value, direction, and a suggested question for a clinician. The supplement view organizes what the user already recorded; it never recommends starting or stopping anything.”

Show **الفحوصات الماضية** with the LDL trend and reference, then **جدول المكملات**.

## 1:42–2:05 — Cardiology cross-signal prompt

**Voiceover:** “This is where Sihha already helped me personally. In my private prototype, it brought together elevated jogging heart-rate patterns and lipid results, gave me a clear reason to prepare a cardiology review, and organized a report I could take to the specialist. Codex with GPT-5.6 helped shape that cross-signal prompt and its safety boundary. The public demo recreates the same benefit with synthetic data; it does not diagnose a rhythm or explain causation.”

Show **الملف الطبي**, then scroll to the large rendered first-page PDF preview. Pause on the visible jogging peak, LDL trend, IF/AND/THEN safety rule, and clinician questions. Open the full synthetic PDF briefly.

## 2:05–2:25 — Brief and privacy

**Voiceover:** “Sihha creates an Arabic or English evidence brief that the user can print, copy, or download. Nothing is shared automatically. The demo is synthetic, and real imports stay in browser memory.”

Return to the dashboard, show the specialist report builder, and point to the synthetic-data and local-processing language.

## 2:25–2:50 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Voiceover:** “Codex with GPT-5.6 helped transform a private personal prototype into a reusable contest product: isolating sensitive data, implementing the Apple Health parser, designing the explanation engine, defining medical safety boundaries, and building automated privacy and rendering tests. Sihha helps people use the data they already own for a better health conversation.”

End on the original-style Sihha dashboard, brand, and repository URL.
